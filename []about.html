Essential Need for Blood

Every day, hospitals require approximately 36,000 units of red blood cells, 7,000 units of platelets, and 10,000 units of plasma. Despite medical advances, there's no artificial substitute for human blood. Regular donations are crucial to maintain adequate supplies for:

  • Cancer treatments
  • Trauma emergencies
  • Surgical procedures
  • Chronic anemia management
  • Transplant operations

Donation Protocol

The FDA-regulated donation process ensures maximum safety:

  1. Pre-Screening: Confidential health questionnaire and mini-physical (10-15 mins)
  2. Phlebotomy: Sterile single-use equipment collection (8-10 mins)
  3. Recovery: Post-donation refreshments and monitoring (15-20 mins)
